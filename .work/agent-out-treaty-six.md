## Maskwacis Four Nations (Treaty 6, central AB)

- The Four Nations of Maskwacis — **Samson Cree Nation, Ermineskin Cree Nation, Louis Bull Tribe, Montana First Nation** — all parties to Treaty 6 (Plains Cree adhesion 1876-08-23, Fort Carlton), all members of the Confederacy of Treaty No. 6.
- Located ~70 km south of Edmonton at Hwy 2A / Hwy 611. Renamed from "Hobbema" in 2014.
- Joint governance vehicle: Maskwacîs Education Schools Commission (MESC), co-owned by all four Nations.
- **Oil and gas exposure (Pigeon Lake + Samson reserves):** Ermineskin is "one of the more economically stable First Nation communities in Canada" because of historic oil/gas royalty income. Under Treaty 6 + the Indian Act, subsurface oil/gas rights were entrusted to the federal Crown for management.
- **Samson v Canada / Ermineskin v Canada (SCC 2009)** — landmark royalty-trust litigation:
  - Samson claim filed 1989; Ermineskin claim filed 1992. Underlying dispute = 1973–1985 royalties, "made-in-Canada" NEP oil-price regime.
  - Royalties were deposited to the federal Consolidated Revenue Fund (CRF) and paid back at 3–6% interest, not invested in a diversified portfolio.
  - Combined legal costs estimated >$100M — among the longest and costliest civil actions in Canadian history.
  - Unanimous 7-judge SCC panel (Rothstein J.): **Crown has neither the power nor the duty to invest Indian monies** held in trust. Crown not liable for failing to diversify.
  - Out-of-court settlement: **$350M transferred to an independent Samson Cree-controlled trust fund**.
- **Bill 14 / Alberta independence litigation (2026, ongoing):** Confederacy of Treaty No. 6 — incl. Samson, Ermineskin, Louis Bull, Montana — joined Sturgeon Lake Cree Nation, Blackfoot Confederacy, and Athabasca Chipewyan FN in legal proceedings against Alberta. Alberta KB Justice Shaina Leonard quashed the Stay Free Alberta independence petition; AB Justice ruled provincial separation without informed consent violates Treaty rights. Confederacy position: *"Alberta sovereignty is an illusion ... The Treaties came first. The 'prosperity' separatists talk about is built on Treaty and the wealth of our lands."*

## Onion Lake Cree Nation (Treaty 6, AB/SK border)

- ~60,000-acre reserve straddling the AB/SK border, 50 km N of Lloydminster. 7,081 band members; ~4,200 on-reserve.
- **Onion Lake Energy** — 100% First Nation-owned company holding oil/gas permits on Nation land.
  - In oil production since 2003 (initially via International Petroleum Corporation JV).
  - ~120 producing wells, ~6,000 bbl/day.
  - Current operators: BlackPearl, Canadian Natural Resources Ltd. (CNRL).
  - Revenue structure: **50% of per-barrel sale price + royalties** (atypical — most FN benefit-sharing arrangements stop at royalties or much smaller equity).
- **Onion Lake Gas Limited Partnership** — the first natural-gas company owned/operated by a First Nation in Saskatchewan, created via a **1996 agreement with Indian Oil & Gas Canada (IOGC)**.
- Strategic significance for the Treaty-Lab thesis: **direct counter-example to the Coastal GasLink forfeiture pattern.** Onion Lake demonstrates that operator-side equity + production control is achievable on Treaty 6 land when the Nation establishes its own corporate vehicle pre-development — rather than being offered a minority stake in someone else's expansion round.

## Saskatchewan Treaty 6 — SaskPower / renewables / water

- **Coverage gap is real:** the SK renewables equity boom is concentrated on **Treaty 4** Nations, not Treaty 6.
  - **Mino Giizis Solar** (Neoen + Anishinabek Power Alliance): 157 MWp / 100 MWac, 25-year SaskPower PPA signed 2026-01, **50% Indigenous co-ownership**, COD 2028. Partner Nations — Zagime Anishinabek, Kinistin Saulteaux, Cote, Key — are **all Treaty 4**.
  - **Pesâkâstêw Solar** (10 MW, Weyburn, online 2022, first grid-scale SK solar with FNPA): partners are George Gordon Developments Ltd. (George Gordon FN) and Red Dog Holdings (Star Blanket Cree Nation) — both **Treaty 4** by Nation, despite one source labelling the project "Treaty 6" (location-vs-Nation ambiguity worth resolving against canonical maps).
- **No major SK Treaty 6 Nation-led renewables-equity PPA with SaskPower is yet visible** in the public record — a stark contrast with the AB Treaty 8 (Astisiy / AII / Stonlasec8) and SK Treaty 4 (Mino Giizis) precedents.
- **SaskPower–FNCoE MOU (2025)** committed to advancing Indigenous participation in the power sector — execution-level Treaty-6 equity transactions have not yet followed in the public record.
- **North–South Transmission Project (approved 2026-03)** will link SaskPower's northern and southern grids — affects Treaty 6 territory in central SK and Treaty 10 in the north. Equity / consultation structure not yet disclosed.
- **Water rights:** *Samson Cree* (Treaty 6) is named alongside *Tsuu T'ina* (Treaty 7) in case law triggering the Crown's duty to consult on the SSRB Plan — the "low threshold" trigger language. Beyond that, SK Treaty 6 water entitlements are addressed almost entirely through **Treaty Land Entitlement (TLE)** instruments rather than as water-rights claims per se — a structural difference from the AB Treaty 7 SSRB-Regulation entitlements (Blood/Piikani/Siksika) and a possible doctrinal gap.
